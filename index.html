IIT interviewer style: He won't just ask definitions. He'll give you a query and ask what's wrong, or give a business problem and ask you to write SQL live. Think out loud every time.
Stay grounded: Say "I've worked with" not "I'm an expert in." He will respect honesty + curiosity over fake expertise. Don't claim things you can't back up.
This is your strongest area. Own it confidently. Reference your actual resume metrics — DSO reduction, classification accuracy, stakeholder count.
Keep it grounded: Don't claim ML expertise. You used Python for Pandas-based analysis and anomaly detection. That's enough — be specific and honest.
STAR method always: Situation → Task → Action → Result. Every behavioral answer needs a number or outcome at the end. Vague answers = weak impression.
